# Metis Web Front-Facing Foundation

This document explains the product from the website side so future front-facing improvements stay aligned with the real product.

## What the website should communicate

Metis helps teams understand what their frontend is costing them.

The website should explain that clearly, then route people into the right account and beta flow. It should not try to impersonate the extension experience.

## Core website role

Metis Web is responsible for:

- product explanation
- trust and framing
- sign up and sign in
- onboarding and account access
- security and legal surfaces
- beta posture and account-managed Plus access

The website is not the scan engine. It is the product story and account layer around that engine.

## Product truth the website should preserve

- Metis reads the current page, not a synthetic benchmark in a vacuum
- the extension owns scanning, report reading, and extension settings
- the website owns account settings, security, legal acceptance, and beta access
- the report should feel specific, grounded, and useful
- the product is about cost visibility and fix order, not generic performance theater

## How to use Metis

The website should explain the real product flow clearly:

- create or access your account on the website
- install and open the extension on a real page
- let Metis read the current route
- move from the side panel into the full report when more explanation is needed
- return to the website for account, security, and beta access

Future front-facing work should keep that split visible instead of turning the site into a fake substitute for the extension.

## What Metis scans

The website should describe scanning in grounded terms.

Metis reads the current route and looks at signals such as:

- request activity
- repeated or duplicate loading patterns
- heavy assets
- third-party script pressure
- AI-style request behavior
- stack and provider clues
- route-level signals that influence cost risk

Public copy should stay honest: Metis reads live page behavior and frames likely cost pressure. It does not claim perfect billing knowledge from the outside.

## How Metis should describe fixes

The website should frame fixes as ranked, useful, and believable.

Good front-facing fix language:

- identifies what is likely happening
- explains why it matters
- points to what should be fixed first
- keeps savings language approximate
- avoids blame-heavy or fake-precision wording

## Current insights and suggestions

What the product can already speak about in a front-facing way:

- top drivers
- cost-risk framing
- control and justification
- likely waste sources
- ranked fixes
- estimate ranges
- missing-context questions that improve the read

Future landing and product pages should describe these as current capabilities, not as future promises.

## What is still missing

What should still be described carefully:

- broader calibration proof across more live sites
- a fully mature export and report-sharing story
- the final website-to-extension entitlement bridge
- any fully live Plus Beta or API Beta behavior beyond staged account posture

This is where the site should stay honest and avoid overstating completeness.

## What the report button means

Front-facing copy should treat the report button as the moment the user goes from summary to full explanation.

It means:

- open the deeper route-specific read
- see the ranked issue story in full
- move toward export and future sharing
- understand the page with more context than the side panel alone

## Who the product is for

- frontend teams
- product engineers
- founders and technical leads
- people who know pages can be expensive but do not have a clean way to explain why

## What future front-facing work should push

- clearer proof that the estimates and issue reads are believable
- stronger storytelling around ranked fixes and report output
- better explanation of how the extension and website work together
- more confidence-building examples around real-page cost risk, not abstract metrics
- a cleaner path from beta interest to account access to real product use

## What future front-facing work should avoid

- treating Metis like a generic monitoring platform
- making the website sound more finished than the real entitlement and beta systems
- flattening the product into vague SaaS language
- hiding the difference between website account management and extension runtime behavior
- overpromising exact cost math or fully live Plus/API entitlement if it is still staged

## Front-facing improvement opportunities

- hero and product framing that make the cost-risk category feel obvious
- better scan/report examples that feel like real product evidence
- stronger export and report-sharing story once that output is real
- final SEO and OG polish that matches the real product voice
- tighter legal and trust language that feels clear instead of defensive

## Simple message ladder

- short line: Metis shows what your frontend is costing you
- medium line: Metis helps teams see cost-risk on real pages and understand what to fix first
- long line: Metis is a website and browser-extension product that helps teams understand where frontend spend starts, why it matters, and how to fix the waste that scales quietly
